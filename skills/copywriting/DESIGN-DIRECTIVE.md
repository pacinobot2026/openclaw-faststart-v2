# 🎨 DESIGN DIRECTIVE

**Design is not decoration. Design controls attention, hierarchy, and emotional intensity.**

Every section must answer:
1. What do I look at first?
2. What do I feel?
3. What do I do next?

That becomes the filter.

---

## 1️⃣ ABOVE THE FOLD MUST CONTAIN:

- ✅ **One dominant headline**
- ✅ **One dominant CTA**
- ✅ **One proof anchor**
- ✅ **One visual hierarchy break**

**NO:**
- ❌ Clutter
- ❌ Secondary CTAs
- ❌ Competing elements

---

## 2️⃣ VISUAL HIERARCHY RULES

### Font Sizing:
- **Headline:** 2.2–2.8x body text
- **Subhead:** 1.3–1.6x body text
- **CTA:** Most saturated color on page

### Background:
- **Calm. Dark or neutral.**

---

## 3️⃣ SECTION RHYTHM RULE

**Alternate:**
- Dark → Lighter → Framed Box → Open Space → Tension Section → Offer Section

**NEVER stack identical section backgrounds twice.**

Create visual variety and movement through the page.

---

## 4️⃣ PSYCHOLOGICAL DESIGN TRIGGERS

Use these intentionally:

| Trigger | Purpose | How |
|---------|---------|-----|
| **Contrast** | Urgency | Sharp color changes, bold vs light |
| **Framing boxes** | Authority | Border sections with confidence |
| **White space** | Premium feel | Don't crowd, let it breathe |
| **Large numbers** | Certainty | 37, $17, 59x — make them BIG |
| **Asymmetry** | Movement | Break perfect grids, create flow |
| **Tight copy blocks** | Intensity | Condensed text = focus |

---

## DESIGN CHECKLIST - Before Publishing:

✅ **Above the fold:**
- [ ] One clear headline (2.2-2.8x body)
- [ ] One CTA visible immediately
- [ ] One proof element
- [ ] Clean, uncluttered

✅ **Section rhythm:**
- [ ] No two identical backgrounds in a row
- [ ] Dark/light/framed variety throughout
- [ ] Visual breaks between sections

✅ **Hierarchy:**
- [ ] Headline is dominant
- [ ] Subheads are 1.3-1.6x body
- [ ] CTA is most saturated color
- [ ] Important numbers are LARGE

✅ **Psychological triggers:**
- [ ] Contrast used for urgency
- [ ] Boxes frame authority
- [ ] White space creates premium feel
- [ ] Asymmetry creates movement

---

**If every section looks the same, you're losing attention. Fix the rhythm.**
