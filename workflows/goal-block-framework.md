## Classification (Do This First)

Before writing ANYTHING, classify the lesson:

| Type | Focus | Output |
|------|-------|--------|
| **Tactical/Software** | How to do something | 4-8 execution steps |
| **Strategic Framework** | Big picture concept | Planning exercise |
| **Conceptual/Mindset** | Mental shift | Reflection + epiphany prompts |
| **Hybrid** | Mix | Steps + reflection |

---

## The Rules (Non-Negotiable)

1. **NO homework vibes** — This is FUN, not school
2. **5th grade reading level** — If a kid can't understand it, rewrite it
3. **Couple things MAX** — NOT a to-do list
4. **NO forecasting next lesson** — That's the instructor's job
5. **NO fabricating** — Only reference what was ACTUALLY taught
6. **Reinforcement must MATCH the lesson** — Not generic praise

---

## The Structure

### Part 1: "Here's the deal"
Reinforce the BIG breakthrough they just learned. Make it click.

### Part 2: "Your one thing"  
ONE simple action. Couple of ideas max. Easy win.

### Part 3: "Drop a comment below"
Engagement questions that are SPECIFIC to what they just learned.

---

## Example (Newsletter 2.0 Lesson)

```
Here's the deal:

You just learned something that changes EVERYTHING.

Articles aren't just blog posts. They're little traffic machines that work 
for you 24/7 — showing up on Google, bringing in subscribers, AND filling 
your newsletter at the same time.

One piece of content. Two jobs. Way less work.

Your one thing:

Think of 3 things happening in your town right now that people might Google.

A new restaurant? A road closure? A local hero? Something funny on the 
community Facebook page?

That's it. Just 3 ideas.

Now drop a comment below! 👇

Did it click for you? Can you see how one article does TWO jobs now? 
What's the first topic YOU want to write about in your town?

We wanna hear it!
```

---

## Bad vs Good Reinforcement

**Generic (BAD):**
- "Are you loving the course?"
- "How excited are you?"
- "What do you think?"

**Lesson-Specific (GOOD):**
- "Can you see how one article does TWO jobs now?"
- "Did the 70/30 split click for you?"
- "What's YOUR first topic gonna be?"

---

## The Vibe Check

Before submitting, ask yourself:
- Would a 5th grader understand this?
- Does it feel like FUN or HOMEWORK?
- Is there momentum to keep going?
- Are the questions specific to THIS lesson?

If any answer is wrong, rewrite it.

---

## Process for Generating Goal Blocks

1. Get the transcript (Vimeo API or provided)
2. Classify the lesson type
3. Extract: core promise, demonstrated outcome, key breakthrough
4. Write using the structure above
5. Vibe check before delivery

**Remember:** "Curriculum intelligence" not "format generator"
